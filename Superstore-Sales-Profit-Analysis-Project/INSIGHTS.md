Superstore Sales & Profit Analysis – Insights

1. Overall Business Performance

The business generated total sales of $2.30M, showing strong revenue generation.

However, total profit stands at $286K with a profit margin of only 12%, indicating limited profitability despite high sales.

2. Category-Level Insights

Technology category is the top performer in both sales and profit.

Furniture category contributes significantly to sales but shows weak profitability.

Office Supplies maintain moderate performance with stable profit contribution.

3. Regional Performance

West region leads in both sales and profit, making it the strongest market.

East region performs well but slightly below West.

Central and South regions show weaker performance, indicating regional imbalance.

4. Discount Impact

Low discount range (0–10%) contributes the highest profit.

Discounts above 20% are strongly associated with reduced or negative profit.

Aggressive discounting is a major factor behind loss-making transactions.

5. Loss-Making Areas

Certain sub-categories such as Tables, Bookcases, and Supplies are consistently loss-making.

States like Texas, Ohio, and Pennsylvania contribute the highest losses.

These areas are significantly impacting overall business profitability.

6. Customer Behavior

A small group of customers contributes a large share of total sales.

Majority of customers fall into low order frequency (1–10 orders).

This indicates limited repeat purchasing behavior and dependency on key customers.

7. Profit & Growth Drivers

Technology category acts as the primary profit driver.

High-performing sub-categories include Phones and Accessories.

West region contributes the highest share of profit.

Business performs better with lower discount strategies.

8. Key Business Gaps

Low profit margin despite strong sales volume.

Over-reliance on discounts to drive sales.

Poor performance in certain regions and sub-categories.

Limited customer retention and repeat purchases.

9. Strategic Focus Areas

Improve profit margins by controlling discounts.

Focus on high-performing categories and regions.

Reduce dependency on loss-making products.

Strengthen customer retention strategies to increase lifetime value.
