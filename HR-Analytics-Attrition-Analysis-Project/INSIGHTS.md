# HR Analytics Attrition Analysis – Key Insights

1. Overview  
This dashboard analyzes employee attrition patterns to identify key factors influencing employee turnover. The objective is to understand workforce behavior, highlight risk areas, and support data-driven retention strategies.

2. Workforce Summary  
Total Employees: 1,470  
Employees Left: 237  
Attrition Rate: 16%  
Average Salary: $7K  
Average Income (Employees Who Left): $5K  

The attrition rate of 16% indicates a moderate but concerning level of employee turnover.

3. Role and Department Insights  
Laboratory Technician, Sales Executive, and Research roles show the highest attrition.  
Research & Development department has the highest number of employee exits.  
Sales department also contributes significantly to overall attrition.  

Attrition is concentrated in specific roles and departments rather than evenly distributed.

4. Demographic Insights  
Employees aged 26–35 show the highest attrition, indicating mid-career turnover.  
Male employees show slightly higher attrition compared to female employees.  
Workforce distribution is 60% male and 40% female.  

Certain demographic segments are more prone to leaving.

5. Compensation Insights  
Employees who left have lower average income compared to overall workforce.  
Low and medium income groups show higher attrition rates.  
Employees receiving lower salary hikes (0–12%) are more likely to leave.  

Compensation plays a significant role in employee retention.

6. Work Environment and Satisfaction  
Employees working overtime show higher attrition.  
Poor work-life balance strongly correlates with employee exits.  
Lower job satisfaction levels lead to higher attrition.  

Work conditions and satisfaction directly impact retention.

7. Experience and Tenure Insights  
Employees with 0–5 years of experience show higher attrition.  
Employees with short tenure (0–2 years) are more likely to leave.  
Attrition decreases among highly experienced employees.  

Early-career employees are at higher risk of leaving.

8. Promotion and Growth Insights  
Employees with longer promotion gaps show higher attrition.  
Delayed promotions contribute to dissatisfaction and exits.  

Career growth opportunities significantly influence retention.

9. Key Takeaways  
Attrition is concentrated in specific roles, departments, and experience levels.  
Compensation, work-life balance, and job satisfaction are major drivers of attrition.  
Mid-career and early-tenure employees are most vulnerable.  
Lack of timely promotions increases turnover risk.  

10. Business Impact  
High attrition increases hiring and training costs.  
Loss of skilled employees affects productivity and business continuity.  
There is a need for targeted retention strategies based on data insights.  
HR analytics can be leveraged to proactively reduce attrition.
